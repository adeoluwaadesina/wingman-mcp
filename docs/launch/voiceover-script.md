# Wingman - Explainer Voiceover Script

Timed to the animated explainer (`docs/launch/` build, ~55 seconds, 6 scenes).
Record this in ElevenLabs, then lay it over your screen recording of the explainer.

## Voice + delivery

- **Suggested voice:** a warm, confident, mid-register narrator. Not hyped, not sleepy. Think "capable co-pilot."
- **ElevenLabs settings (starting point):** Stability 45-55, Similarity 75, Style 0-15, Speaker Boost on. Model: Multilingual v2 or the latest English model.
- **Pace:** unhurried. Let the on-screen motion breathe. The timings below have slack built in; do not rush to fill silence.
- **Total target:** ~52-55 seconds of speech across ~56 seconds of video.

## Script (with scene timings)

Each block is one scene. The bracketed time is when the scene starts on screen. Leave a short beat of silence at the very start (logo draw) before the first line.

---

**[0:02] Scene 1 - Brand open**
> This is Wingman. An interactive plan panel that lives right inside your AI chat.

**[0:07] Scene 2 - The panel**
> Give it a plan, and it turns your conversation into a living checklist. One you and your assistant share.

**[0:16] Scene 3 - In action**
> Add tasks. Check them off. Mark what's in flight. Everything updates live, as the work gets done.

**[0:27] Scene 4 - Sync**
> And because Wingman runs in the cloud, your plans follow you. Laptop, phone, any AI that speaks MCP. Tick it in one place, it's ticked everywhere.

**[0:39] Scene 5 - Connect in 3 steps**
> Connecting takes about thirty seconds. Add a custom connector, paste the Wingman URL, and sign in. Your plans are already there.

**[0:51] Scene 6 - Close**
> Wingman. Your plans, synced everywhere. Get started at wingman dash m-c-p dot onrender dot com.

---

## Notes

- "MCP" is read as letters: "em-see-pee."
- The closing URL is read slowly and clearly: "wingman dash m-c-p dot onrender dot com." The URL is also on screen in Scene 6, so the viewer can read along.
- If a line lands long against its scene, prefer trimming the SYNC line (Scene 4) - it has the most words. Keep the connect steps (Scene 5) intact; that is the call to action.
- No em dashes in delivery; the hyphens above are just pauses.

## Alternate shorter close (if you want a 45s cut)

Drop Scene 3's second sentence and use this close:
